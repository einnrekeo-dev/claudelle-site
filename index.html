Written in the quiet hours when missing you became too loud for silence alone.

Chapter One
On Loving in Secret
The Poems You'll Never Read

I've always wrote you in poems you'll never read. I kept your name in the folds of my quietest nights, where the world could not hear me loving you.

I let every thought of you become a line, every ache become a stanza, every silence become a page. You lived in my words long before you ever lived in my life. I wrote you as if paper could carry what my mouth could not say — as if ink could become courage, as if poetry could find its way to you when I could not.

✦
There are feelings too fragile for confession, too tender for daylight. So I hid them in metaphors, in soft beginnings and unfinished endings, in the kind of verses that tremble like a held breath. I loved you in secret, and my poems became the only place that love was free.
✦

You held a version of me that still hoped, still waited, still believed that maybe one day you would turn the page and finally know. You were still there in every poem, every pause, every piece of me that could not let go.

Chapter Two
On Choosing You
I Chose to Yearn for You

I still love you — even without conversation again, even if we act like strangers. I still want you to come back. I hope one day we can try again.

"You wanted me, but never kept me. You wanted me — and I wanted you too. We felt the same thing. We had something real. But somehow, that still wasn't enough."

Because I chose you fully, completely, without hesitation. And you… you only wanted me until it got hard to stay. I gave you everything I could — every part of me that knew how to love. But it still wasn't enough to make you keep me. And that's what hurts the most — not that you didn't feel anything, but that you felt it, and still let me go.

✦

I would have stayed, even when things got difficult. I would have stayed through all of it. But you — you wanted me, just not enough to keep me.

I wished I was enough. I know you wouldn't settle for less. And my intention is still the same — to treat you the way you've always deserved.

Chapter Three
On What I Failed to Give
The Flowers I Should Have Brought

I know I wasn't the person who planned the dates. I know there were flowers that needed to be asked for, that shouldn't have to be asked for. I know you waited — for messages, for updates, for the little things that let someone know they're thought of. And I was quiet when I should have spoken.

Busyness was never a good enough reason. It never is. A person who is waiting for you deserves more than silence. They deserve to know they are still held in your mind while you carry the world. I know that now. I wish I'd known it then.

✦
"Consistency speaks louder than both words and actions. And I wasn't consistent. That is my fault — not yours."

There were moments I settled into a version of myself that wasn't worthy of you — not because I stopped loving you, but because my life was falling at the same time, and I didn't know how to hold both. I let the weight of everything pull me under, and in doing so, I let go of small moments that mattered more than I realized.

I'm sorry for every time you waited. I'm sorry for every flower that was never brought without asking. I'm sorry for every silence that felt like distance, even when my heart had not moved an inch from you.

Chapter Four
On What Was Never True
The Thing That Never Happened

I need you to know — I did not betray you. What you believed happened, did not happen. I did not go to someone else. I did not choose another. The fear that I might have, the thought that I was capable of hurting you that way — I understand why it lived in your mind. Overthinking is its own kind of pain, and I know yours came from a place of caring.

But I want to give you the answers. Not to win, not to be right — but because you deserve clarity where there was confusion. Everything that felt like a sign of something wrong was only misread in a painful moment. And painful moments have a way of making the untrue feel certain.

✦
"I want to stand in the rain with you. I want to give you the truth that sets down the weight you've been carrying."

What separated us was not the presence of betrayal. It was the absence of enough communication, enough reassurance, enough of me showing up in the small ways that would have stopped the fear from growing. That is the real answer. And I am sorry it took this much distance to say it clearly.

I know you know where I am. I know you know how to reach me. Whenever you are ready — if you are ever ready — I will be here.

Chapter Five
On Knowing You
Everything I Carry of You

Let me tell you what I see, because I see everything.

I know you love purple — and bell flowers, those soft little hanging things that look like the color of dusk.
I know you love attention given with love — not performed, but felt. The kind that makes you soften.
I know you love cookies and cream ice cream. I know that small sweetness matters to you.
I know when things get hard, you still do them. You carry your stress quietly and push through anyway.
I love your eyes when sunlight hits them and shows that dark brown underneath — that color is my favorite now. My favorite thing to admire.
I love when your eyes smile before your mouth does. I have wished so many times that I could kiss them.
✦

From now on — whenever I think of something pretty, I will count you first. You are the measure of beautiful things.

Chapter Six
On Missing You
I Miss Us the Most

I miss our late-night talks that lasted until we didn't notice the time. The random conversations that made no sense but felt like the most important thing in the world. I miss the laughter on calls — the kind that came from nowhere and turned into something neither of us could stop.

I miss simpleng kumain kasama ka — something as ordinary as a meal that became everything just because you were there. I miss the chikahan. The inside jokes. The comfort of knowing that at the end of the day, I had you to talk to.

✦
I catch myself scrolling back through old messages, smiling at what we used to be, wishing I could call you just like before. I need you to need me back. I know we can still fix this. I wish you would let us try.
✦

Even in the middle of your conscious decision to step away — I understand it. I don't fight it. I only hope that understanding can find its way back to you too. And I hope you know: whenever you're ready, nothing in me will have moved.

Chapter Seven
On What I Promise
What I Wish For Us

If we ever find our way back — and I still believe we might — here is what I carry as truth for us:

I wish we bring up small things early, calmly — because resentment builds when silence becomes a habit.
I wish we notice what actually makes each other soften — not just what we think love looks like, but what lands.
I wish we keep doing the small things consistently. Relationships don't fail from one big moment. They fade when effort becomes occasional instead of normal.
I wish we remember — it's not you versus me. It's both of us, together, versus the problem.
I wish we give space without creating tension. Sometimes people need to reset, not disconnect. Distance is not always disinterest.
I promise to update you. To never let you wait in silence for hours wondering. You will always know where I am.
"I'm not perfect. But I promise to always give my best — to be honest and open with you, and to never give up on us or make you question how deeply you are loved."

Final Chapter
On Everything That Remains
Still Here. Still Yours.

I know my pain took over sometimes and made me forget the pain you were carrying. I'm sorry for every moment my silence made you feel like you didn't matter. You matter. You have always mattered — more than I knew how to say in the moments it counted most.

I have never wanted anyone the way I want you. Not in the casual sense. In the kind of way where you realize — why would I look for someone better, when you are already enough for me? You are not a placeholder. You are not a convenience. You are the person I chose fully, and I would choose you again.

✦
I could never really love another soul the way I love yours. From the bottom of my heart — that is not poetry. That is just the truth.
✦

Please hug me when you see me. Please show me the love you've been hiding — love it loudly with me. Tell me when things go wrong. Open to me when things get difficult.

I will be here. I have been here. I am not going anywhere.

— with all of me, always 💜
